# Potential Enhancements

- **Third triangulation method**: add a "value-based" TAM estimate (e.g., derived from the
  cost savings/productivity value AI retrieval infrastructure creates, captured as a % of
  that value) as a third independent cross-check alongside top-down and bottom-up.
- **Sensitivity on category attribution %**: since the top-down method's biggest uncertainty
  is the % of enterprise software spend attributable to this category, build an explicit
  sensitivity table across a range of plausible attribution percentages.
- **Named competitor profiles**: once live data access is available, replace the generic
  competitor categories with named companies and their actual funding/market position for a
  real competitive analysis.
- **Geographic segmentation**: break TAM/SAM into major geographies (North America, Europe,
  APAC) separately, since go-to-market strategy and realistic SOM often differ significantly
  by region.
- **S-curve adoption modeling**: replace the constant annual growth rate assumption with an
  S-curve (slow early adoption, rapid middle-phase growth, eventual saturation) more typical
  of real technology category adoption.
- **Customer segment breakdown**: split the bottom-up estimate by company size (SMB,
  mid-market, enterprise) with different adoption rates and spend levels per segment.
- **Link to a specific company's SOM capture plan**: connect this market sizing directly to
  [`VC/investment-memo-startup`](../investment-memo-startup) to show how a specific company's
  revenue projections map to a % of the SOM modeled here over time.

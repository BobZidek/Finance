# Investment Memo — Vertex Retrieval (Series A)

**Target**: Vertex Retrieval (hypothetical vector database / RAG infrastructure startup) |
**Round**: Series A | **Proposed Investment**: $12mm at $48mm pre-money ($60mm post-money)

*This memo's narrative sections are illustrative and general — written to demonstrate the
structure and content of a real VC investment memo, not to make claims about any real
company. The quantitative exhibits are generated by `code/investment_memo_model.py`; see
`output/` for the underlying tables and charts.*

## 1. Executive Summary

Vertex Retrieval is a vector database / retrieval infrastructure startup with **$2.4mm ARR
across 24 enterprise customers**, **118% NRR**, and a **1.1x burn multiple** — early but
promising unit economics for a company at this stage (see
[`VC/saas-metrics-dashboard`](../saas-metrics-dashboard) for how these specific metrics should
be read). The proposed Series A values the company at **25.0x current ARR**, a rich multiple
reflecting strong category momentum in AI infrastructure (see Section 2). Base-case exit
scenario returns **4.33x MOIC / 27.7% IRR**; the bull case (consistent with capturing a
meaningful share of the category's SOM, see
[`VC/market-sizing-ai-infra`](../market-sizing-ai-infra)) returns **16.25x / 59.2% IRR**; the
bear case is a **modest loss (0.87x MOIC)** — a realistic downside given typical early-stage
loss rates (see [`VC/portfolio-power-law-model`](../portfolio-power-law-model)).

## 2. Market Analysis

Vertex Retrieval operates in the vector database / AI retrieval infrastructure category sized
in [`VC/market-sizing-ai-infra`](../market-sizing-ai-infra): a market projected to grow from a
**$19.75bn midpoint TAM (triangulated top-down/bottom-up) in 2026 to $68.9bn by 2031**, with a
**$24.1bn SAM** and a **~$241mm SOM** representing a realistic top-decile single-company
outcome. This memo's **Bull exit scenario ($1.5bn)** is calibrated to be broadly consistent
with a company capturing that SOM at a market-appropriate revenue multiple — i.e., the
scenario isn't an arbitrary optimistic number, it's grounded in the category-level market
sizing work. The competitive landscape flags two "High" threat categories (hyperscaler-native
offerings and other specialized startups) — Vertex's differentiation thesis needs to
specifically address both, not just one.

## 3. Team Assessment (illustrative)

A real memo's team section would assess: technical depth relevant to the specific engineering
challenges of the category (query performance at scale, embedding pipeline integration),
prior experience selling developer-facing infrastructure into enterprises, and completeness of
the go-to-market function relative to the stage. Typical diligence questions an IC would want
answered before a Series A: has the team previously built and scaled infrastructure software,
what's the actual mix of inbound vs. outbound in current customer acquisition, and is there a
credible plan (and early hires) for the enterprise sales motion this ARR level will require to
sustain growth into the next round.

## 4. Key Risk Factors

- **Premium entry multiple**: 25.0x current ARR is a rich valuation reflecting the category's
  current momentum, not the company's revenue scale alone — if AI infrastructure valuations
  broadly compress before the next round, this could pressure both the mark and the ability to
  raise a strong Series B at an up-round.
- **Competitive intensity**: as flagged in the market sizing exhibit, both hyperscaler-native
  offerings and other well-funded specialized startups represent genuine "High" threats — this
  is a category where technical differentiation can erode quickly if a larger platform ships a
  "good enough" native alternative.
- **Future dilution**: this memo's return scenarios already account for an assumed 65%
  retention ratio (roughly two more financing rounds at ~20% dilution each) — if the company
  needs to raise more capital, or on worse terms, than assumed, effective ownership (and
  therefore realized returns) would be lower than modeled here.
- **Concentration/customer risk**: at only 24 enterprise customers, losing even one or two
  large accounts could materially affect both revenue and the retention metrics underpinning
  this memo's optimism.

## 6. Recommendation

Proceed to a full diligence process, with particular focus on (1) validating the durability of
the 118% NRR across a larger sample as the customer base grows past 24 accounts, and (2)
building specific conviction on Vertex's defensibility against hyperscaler-native alternatives
— the single biggest risk to the bull case materializing. The base-case return profile (4.33x
MOIC / 27.7% IRR) clears a typical early-stage venture return threshold, and the bull case is
grounded in defensible category-level market sizing rather than an arbitrary optimistic
assumption, supporting continued pursuit of this investment subject to diligence findings on
team execution and competitive positioning.

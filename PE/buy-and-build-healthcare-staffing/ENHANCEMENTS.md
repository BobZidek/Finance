# Potential Enhancements

- **Standalone platform comparison**: run the platform alone (no bolt-ons) through the same
  exit-multiple assumption and quantify exactly how much of the 2.75x MOIC is attributable to
  the buy-and-build strategy versus the platform's own organic growth.
- **Revenue synergies**: add revenue synergies (cross-selling platform's national accounts to
  bolt-on customers, and vice versa) alongside the cost synergies currently modeled, using the
  reduced flow-through convention from the airline merger model.
- **Bolt-on integration costs**: add one-time integration costs per bolt-on (similar to the
  airline merger model) as a cash drag in the acquisition year and the following year.
- **Variable bolt-on multiples/timing**: parameterize the bolt-on pipeline (number, timing,
  size, multiple) so different roll-up paces and pricing environments can be compared.
- **Failed/delayed bolt-on scenario**: model a scenario where a planned bolt-on falls through
  or is delayed, and show the impact on exit EBITDA and returns versus the base case.
- **Individual tranche debt schedules**: replace the combined single debt balance with
  separately amortizing tranches per acquisition (each with its own rate and mandatory
  amortization), for more precision on blended cost of debt over time.
- **Exit multiple sensitivity**: sweep the exit multiple (and the platform-vs-bolt-on multiple
  spread specifically) to show how sensitive returns are to the multiple arbitrage assumption
  holding at exit.

# Potential Enhancements

- **Property-level cap rate sensitivity**: extend the sensitivity table to shift each property
  type's cap rate independently rather than moving all three together, identifying which
  segment's cap rate assumption the NAV conclusion is most sensitive to.
- **AFFO (Adjusted FFO)**: extend FFO to AFFO by subtracting recurring capital expenditures
  (maintenance capex, leasing commissions) — a more conservative, and often more widely used,
  "true cash available for distribution" metric than FFO alone.
- **Same-store NOI growth**: add a forward NOI growth projection by property type and compute
  a forward (not just trailing) NAV, since real estate valuations are often built on
  next-twelve-months NOI rather than trailing.
- **Debt maturity schedule and refinancing risk**: add a debt maturity ladder and interest rate
  assumptions for upcoming refinancings, a material real-world REIT risk factor.
- **Development pipeline value**: add unstabilized/under-development properties valued
  separately (often at cost or a projected stabilized NOI approach) alongside the stabilized
  portfolio.
- **Occupancy and lease rollover risk**: incorporate portfolio occupancy rates and lease
  expiration schedules, since near-term lease rollover risk affects the reliability of current NOI.
- **Real property/market data**: once live data access is available, apply this framework to a
  real REIT's actual property portfolio and reported financials.
